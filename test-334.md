# Latest News: Dubai — March 2026

## Building Safety Law

Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid issued a comprehensive building safety law on March 10, aimed at raising standards across the emirate. Key highlights:

- Requires regular maintenance checks, inspections, and stricter rules for obtaining Quality and Safety Certificates.
- Fines range from **Dh100,000 to Dh1 million**, with repeat violations doubled to **Dh2 million**.
- Applies to all Dubai buildings, including those in private developments and free zones like the Dubai International Financial Centre (DIFC).

## Public Safety Law

A new public safety law was introduced on March 6, setting strict requirements for venues and events:

- Mandates capacity limits, ventilation, lighting, and noise controls.
- Requires mandatory fire-fighting equipment and evacuation plans.
- Penalties range from **Dh500 to Dh1 million**, rising to **Dh2 million** for repeat offences within a year.

## Government Services Outsourcing Law

On March 13, **Law No. (5) of 2026** was issued, allowing private companies to deliver certain public services on behalf of government entities:

- Promotes public-private partnerships while maintaining accountability and transparency.
- Government entities have a **three-year compliance timeline** to align with the new framework.

## Digital Rental Disputes System

On March 17, Dubai's **Rental Disputes Center** and **Dubai Police** launched an integrated digital system to streamline rental dispute resolution:

- Enables faster case handling and real-time transaction monitoring.
- Improves coordination between government departments involved in rental disputes.
