# Latest News: Australia — March 2026

## Fuel Crisis

Prime Minister **Anthony Albanese** has called a second emergency national cabinet meeting to address an escalating fuel crisis driven by the Middle East war:

- Around **470 service stations** across Australia have run out of at least one fuel type.
- Energy Minister **Chris Bowen** confirmed the government has replaced six cancelled oil tankers and secured three additional ones.
- Rationing has been ruled out, though voluntary conservation measures are being considered.
- Rising fuel costs are threatening freight and waste collection services nationwide.

## Iranian Tourist Ban

The federal government has banned over **7,000 Iranian visitor visa holders** from entering Australia for six months, effective March 26:

- Home Affairs Minister **Tony Burke** said visas issued before the Iran conflict may not have been approved under current circumstances.
- Critics including independent MP **Zali Steggall** and refugee advocates have called the move "brutal and unfair."
- Opponents argue it lacks nuance and sets a dangerous precedent for Australia's migration system.

## AFL Illicit Drug Policy

The AFL has announced what CEO **Andrew Dillon** called "the most stringent" drug policy in Australian sport:

- All AFL and AFLW players will undergo **hair testing twice yearly**.
- Replaces the previous three-strikes model.
- Includes mandatory education, treatment, monitoring, and a **2–4 match suspension** for public illicit drug use.

## Northern Territory Flooding

The **Katherine River** peaked just below major flood levels without causing additional damage, offering relief after the region's worst flooding in nearly three decades:

- Remote evacuees are beginning to return home.
- The **Daly River** continues to experience prolonged major flooding, with homes still underwater.
