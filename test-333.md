# Latest News: Iran and Israel — March 2026

## Full-Scale Conflict Escalation

The situation between Iran and Israel has escalated into a full-scale war involving the US-Israel alliance against Iran, which began on February 28, 2026.

## Military Operations

- **Israeli Strikes on Iran (March 26):** Israel launched wide-scale strikes across Iran, targeting infrastructure including the central city of Isfahan.
- **Iranian Missile Attacks:** Iran fired long-range ballistic missiles for the first time in the conflict, including two 4,000-kilometer-range missiles aimed at the US-UK military base at Diego Garcia in the Indian Ocean on March 21.
- **Strikes Near Israeli Nuclear Site:** Iranian attacks damaged areas near Israel's nuclear facility in Dimona and Arad on March 21, injuring dozens.
- **Gulf States Targeted:** Iran launched attacks on Gulf states following UN condemnation. The UAE intercepted a ballistic missile over Abu Dhabi, with debris killing two and injuring three. Saudi Arabia intercepted at least 18 drones, while Bahrain, Kuwait, and other Gulf nations also reported incoming attacks.

## Diplomatic Efforts

- The US sent a **15-point settlement proposal** to Iran through Pakistan, addressing Iran's nuclear and missile programs, support for proxy groups, and the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz.
- **Trump** claimed Iran wanted a deal to end the war.
- Iran's Foreign Minister **Abbas Araghchi** rejected direct negotiations, stating Iran sought "an end to the war on our own terms."
- Iran responded negatively to the US proposal and set forth its own **five conditions**, including US-Israeli guarantees against resuming war and compensation for damages.

## Economic Impact

- Oil prices have risen approximately **50%** due to the conflict.
- Iran has largely blocked the **Strait of Hormuz**, through which 20% of global oil transits, creating an unprecedented energy supply shock.
